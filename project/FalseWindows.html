False Windows is a digitally curated collection of windows and people from all walks of life around the bustling community of Shanghai Longtang. It uncovers the unseen ties between these individuals, offering a glimpse into the diverse array of lives within the city.

Captured through photogrammetry, the window acts as a passive observer, witnessing all the happenings within Shanghai from the mundane to the spectacular. From the outside, these residents appear disconnected from each other, but behind the facade, they actually lead very interconnected lives.

Ultimately, this work encourages viewers to look beyond the constraints of small windows and societal divisions, revealing the connections that bind us together.

Collective Memory

Old Longtang Community
Under Demolishing
in Emerging Metropolis

Shanghai Longtang 弄堂, also known as Shanghai Alley or Linong, is a semi-public urban space and resident community centred on a lane or several interconnected lanes. As a kind of real estate project during the concession period, it elegantly blended the vernacular courtyard building in south areas of the Yangtze River and the townhouse prototype from western cultures.

However, with the changes of Chinese political contexts and property rights, most of these Longtang communities are now exist as densely populated slums. As a result of urban renewal in the past decade, these dilapidated communities have been gradually disappearing from the urban environment.

Collective Memory

Record, Represent and Recall
the Vanishing Materiality and Humanity
as Urban Nostalgia

Besides the physical lane space and the historic Shikumen buildings, what also disppearing are the tightly-knit community interpersonal relationships. Such relationships have become increasingly rare in the real estate development since the Reform and Opening-Up, turning into a valuable yet fragile urban nostalgia.

In the materiality stage, the project utilizes photogrammetry to document various Longtang facades, as well as AI-assisted animation techniques to recreate the vivid living scenes, preserving traces of daily life.
By reassemblying these realistic assets alone the narrative of windows —a fundamental architectural element—the film ultimately seeks to awaken the awareness of waning interpersonal connections in contemporary society on the spiritual and humanity level.
